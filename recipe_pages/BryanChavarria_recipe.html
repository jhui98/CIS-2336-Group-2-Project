Banh Mi by Bryan Chavarria

Ingredients

  • 1/2 (12 inch) French baguette
  • 10 oz. pork tenderloin
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1/2 small jalapeno pepper
  • 2 tsp soy sauce - 1 tsp Asian sesame oil
  • 1/2 cup cilantro sprigs
  • 1/2 cup thin sliced cucumber / carrots
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp Sriracha sauce
  • 1/2 tsp Hosin sauce

Instructions

  1. Unpack pork
  2. Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  3. Add onion and saute until soft, about 3 minutes.
  4. Add pork, soy sauce, sesame oil, hosin sauce, onion and garlic powders, and pepper.
  5. Cook, stirring often, until browned, about 5 - 6 minutes.
  6. Remove from heat and set aside.
  7. While the pork rests, lets move onto making the sandwhich!
  8. Turn your broiler on high.
  9. Place the baguettes, cut side up, on a baking sheet, and place under the broiler for about 2 minutes until lightly browned and toasted. Keep an eye on them! Don't let them burn!
  10. On the bottom portion of each baguette: stack the Banh Mi with
  11. Cilantro, Think sliced Cucmbers & Carrots, Seasoned Pork, Drizzle of Soy, Pickled Jalapenos, Drizzle of Chile Oil.
  12. Cut in half, if desired, and serve.
Banh Mi literally means "bread" in Vietnamese; This sandwich is popular around the world, especially in countries with a high concentration of Vietnamese people, from France, Canada, United Kingdom to the United States.
